About The Daily Worker

Our Mission

The Daily Worker delivers accurate, timely news that matters to working-class Americans, analyzed through a Marxist/Leninist lens. We don't shy away from uncomfortable truths, and we don't pull punches when it comes to exposing exploitation, inequality, and injustice.

What We Believe

  • Quality Over Quantity: We publish 3-10 carefully researched articles daily, not a flood of clickbait. Every article meets our standards for accuracy and relevance.
  • Worker-Centric Perspective: All news is analyzed through the lens of working-class interests. We ask "How does this affect workers?" for every story.
  • No Corporate Influence: We're not beholden to advertisers, shareholders, or corporate interests. Our only loyalty is to the truth and to working people.
  • Evidence-Based Analysis: Every article cites multiple credible sources and includes academic citations where appropriate. We value accuracy over ideology.

How We Work

Our content pipeline combines AI-powered discovery and generation with human editorial oversight:

  • Discovery: We monitor RSS feeds, social media, and trusted news sources to identify stories that matter to working people.
  • Filtering: Topics are evaluated for source credibility, worker relevance, and engagement potential before we commit to coverage.
  • Generation: AI tools help draft articles with proper reading level (7.5-8.5 Flesch-Kincaid), always including "Why This Matters" and "What You Can Do" sections.
  • Review: Human editors review every article before publication to ensure accuracy, tone, and quality standards are met.

Content Standards

Every article we publish must meet these criteria:

  • Minimum 3 credible sources cited
  • Reading level between 7.5-8.5 (accessible but not dumbed down)
  • Worker relevance score of at least 0.3
  • Engagement potential score of at least 0.3
  • "Why This Matters" section explaining broader significance
  • "What You Can Do" section providing concrete action steps

AI-Generated Content Disclosure

We use artificial intelligence tools to help discover topics, draft articles, and source images. However, all content is reviewed and edited by human editors before publication. AI is a tool that helps us work more efficiently—it doesn't replace human judgment, fact-checking, or editorial oversight.

Our Principles

  • Accuracy First: We correct mistakes promptly and prominently.
  • No Punches Pulled: We call out exploitation, corruption, and injustice regardless of who's responsible.
  • Worker Solidarity: We support labor organizing, strikes, and collective action.
  • Broad Coverage: We cover Labor, Tech, Politics, Economics, Current Affairs, Art & Culture, Sport, and Good News.
  • Regional Focus: National news and local stories that affect working families.

Contact

We're building this platform to serve working people. If you have story tips, feedback, or want to get involved, we want to hear from you.

Note: The Daily Worker is currently in development. Full contact information will be available when we launch publicly.

History

The Daily Worker honors the legacy of the original Daily Worker newspaper (1924-1958), which covered labor news and working-class struggles during a critical period of American history. Like our predecessor, we're committed to telling the stories that corporate media ignores.
