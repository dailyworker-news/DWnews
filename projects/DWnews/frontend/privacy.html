Privacy Policy

Last Updated: December 29, 2025

Overview

The Daily Worker is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your personal data.

Short version: We collect minimal data, don't sell your information, and don't use invasive tracking. We believe your reading habits are your business, not ours.

Information We Collect

Information You Provide

We currently do NOT require user accounts, so we don't collect:

  • Names, email addresses, or contact information
  • Login credentials or passwords
  • Payment information (the site is free)
  • Personal profiles or preferences

Information Automatically Collected

When you visit our site, we may collect:

  • Server Logs: IP address, browser type, operating system, referring page, pages visited, and timestamps. This data is used for site maintenance and security only.
  • Local Storage: Your selected region preference (National/Local/All) is stored locally in your browser. This never leaves your device.

What We Don't Collect

  • No Cookies: We don't use cookies or tracking cookies.
  • No Third-Party Trackers: No Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or other surveillance tools.
  • No Fingerprinting: We don't use device fingerprinting or browser fingerprinting techniques.
  • No Email Lists: We don't collect email addresses for newsletters or marketing (yet).

How We Use Information

The minimal data we collect is used only for:

  • Site Operation: Delivering content and ensuring the site functions properly
  • Security: Detecting and preventing abuse, spam, or attacks
  • Technical Improvements: Understanding aggregate usage patterns to improve performance

We do NOT use your data for:

  • Advertising or marketing
  • Selling or sharing with third parties
  • Profiling or behavioral analysis
  • Cross-site tracking

Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties, except in these limited circumstances:

  • Legal Compliance: If required by law, court order, or government request
  • Security Threats: To protect against fraud, abuse, or security threats
  • Service Providers: Cloud hosting providers (Google Cloud Platform) who are contractually obligated to protect your data

Data Retention

  • Server Logs: Retained for 30 days, then automatically deleted
  • Local Storage: Retained in your browser until you clear it. We have no access to this data.

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of any personal data we hold (currently none)
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your data (clear your browser's local storage)
  • Opt-Out: Stop visiting the site (we don't track you anyway)

Children's Privacy

The Daily Worker is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can delete it.

Third-Party Links

Our articles may link to external websites (source citations, related content). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites. Check their privacy policies before providing personal information.

Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect data:

  • HTTPS encryption for all connections
  • Secure database access controls
  • Regular security audits and updates
  • No storage of unnecessary personal data

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as our platform evolves. Significant changes will be posted prominently on the site. The "Last Updated" date at the top indicates the most recent revision.

Data Protection Compliance

While we are not currently subject to GDPR (no EU operations) or CCPA (no CA operations), we follow privacy-respecting principles inspired by these regulations:

  • Data minimization (collect only what's necessary)
  • Purpose limitation (use data only for stated purposes)
  • Transparency (clear communication about data practices)
  • User control (easy access and deletion)

Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or want to exercise your data rights, contact us at [contact information will be added before public launch].

Philosophy

We believe surveillance capitalism is exploitation. Corporate media treats readers as products to be tracked, profiled, and sold to advertisers. We reject that model.

You're here to read news, not to be tracked. Your privacy is a right, not a privilege.
